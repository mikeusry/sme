## Strategic Decisions Emerging

1. **Sub vs. Direct**: Mike and Bo agree — don't start as a subcontractor. Go direct to site owners. "Let's just try to go find our own"
2. **Lavonia as resume builder**: Bo sees value even at thin margins — "if we can make it work, it would be a lot more attractive for going after these larger sites if we have it on our resume"
3. **Route efficiency**: If they win Athens + Elberton + Lavonia, they can check 5-6 sites in one day loop
4. **Multi-species approach**: Sheep + goats for vegetation, pigs for root work (kudzu tubers). Chickens/chicken tractors and bees also possible on solar sites (Greg confirmed solar companies open to it)
5. **FFA labor model**: Partner with local FFA chapters for daily wellness checks. Reduces Bo's windshield time. Good for the community story
6. **Nofence/virtual fencing**: GPS collars ideal for solar sites — no physical fencing setup/teardown. Remote monitoring with cameras
7. **Southland cross-sell**: Solar grazing contracts that include mowing = opportunity to sell Southland Organics weed killer
8. **Proximity advantage**: 3 utility-scale sites (1,000+ combined acres) within reasonable range of Bogart
