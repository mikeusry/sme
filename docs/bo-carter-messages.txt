Mike
Site & Access
	•	Panel height/type/spacing — determines if sheep can move freely underneath; some designs are too low
	•	How far is the drive from Bogart? Lavonia is ~70 miles — factor transport costs and check-in frequency
	•	Trailer access — can you get livestock trailers to the site? Road/gate conditions?
	•	Site security — locked gates, who has access, any theft/vandalism history?

Vegetation & Terrain
	•	What's growing now? Species mix, any invasives (e.g., sericea lespedeza, privet)
	•	Target height specs — what does the panel manufacturer or utility require?
	•	Terrain — flat, sloped, drainage issues? Rocky ground that affects fencing?
	•	Has the site been sprayed recently? Residue could harm livestock

Animals & Operations
	•	Who provides the sheep? You bring yours, or are they supplying?
	•	Predator pressure — coyotes, stray dogs in the area? (ties to your dogs question)
	•	Vet access nearby — closest large-animal vet to Lavonia?
	•	Shelter/shade — panels provide some, but any structures for extreme weather?
	•	Rotational plan — who designs the grazing rotation? You or them?
	•	Off-season plan — where do animals go if seasonal? Transport costs both ways?

Contract & Liability
	•	Who's the end client? Utility company, solar developer, landowner? Know the chain
	•	Contract term — 1 year? Multi-year? Renewal terms?
	•	Performance metrics — how do they define "success"? Height thresholds, inspection scores?
	•	Panel damage liability — if a sheep or dog damages a panel, who pays?
	•	Livestock loss liability — if equipment or site conditions injure/kill animals, who bears that?
	•	Termination clause — notice period, early exit penalties?
	•	Exclusivity — can you take other solar grazing contracts nearby?

Subcontractor-Specific (critical)
	•	What does the prime contractor handle vs. you? Clear scope split
	•	Reporting requirements — photos, logs, vegetation measurements?
	•	How often do they expect site visits/updates?
	•	Do they have other subs on site you'd need to coordinate with (mowing crews, electrical techs)?
	•	Their ASGA membership status — are they established or new to solar grazing?

Money Questions to Pin Down
	•	Per-acre rate vs. per-head vs. flat fee — know which model they're proposing
	•	Who covers transport costs?
	•	Who covers fencing setup/maintenance costs?
	•	Water delivery — if no on-site water, who pays for hauling?

The Big-Picture Question
is this a subcontract or a partnership? If they're just looking for cheap labor with sheep, the margins may not be worth 70 miles of driving. If they want a true partner who manages the grazing program, that's a different (better) conversation. The call should clarify which one this is early.
