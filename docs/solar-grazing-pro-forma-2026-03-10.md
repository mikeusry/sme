## Assumptions & Sources

- Pricing range: $400-600/acre/year (ASGA, Cornell, confirmed by Greg Gunthorpe + Bo's contact)
- Stocking rate: 2-4 sheep/acre under utility-scale arrays (ASGA standard; adjusted for forage quality)
- Grazing season: ~8 months in NE Georgia (Mar-Oct), with some winter supplementation
- Lamb crop: 1.5 lambs/ewe average, marketable at ~$200-300/head (regional auction + direct)
- Nofence collars: ~$199/unit + annual subscription (~$10-15/mo estimated)
- Mortality allowance: 3-5% annually (ASGA standard)
- Bo's time value: $50/hr (opportunity cost baseline)

## Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Sheep mortality event (disease, predators) | High — 10-20% flock loss = $20K-40K | Insurance, guardian dogs, vet relationship, meningeal worm protocol |
| Contract non-renewal | High — revenue drops to zero | Multi-site diversification, strong relationships, performance documentation |
| Forage failure (drought) | Medium — supplemental feed costs spike | Drought clause in contracts, feed reserve budget |
| Panel damage liability | Medium — single incident could be $10K+ | Clear contract language, proper insurance, training protocols |
| Transport cost increases | Low-Medium | Prioritize close sites, phase in Lavonia only with cluster |
| Nofence/tech investment timing | Low | Delay until Year 2+ with proven contracts |
